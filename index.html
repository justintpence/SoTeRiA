Socio-Technical Risk Analystd

I am part of a multidisciplinary team of researchers in the Socio-Technical Risk Analysis (SoTeRiA) Laboratory Directed by Prof. Zahra Mohaghegh, and am pursuing a PhD in Informatics being advised by Prof. Mohaghegh. SoTeRiA Laboratory is advancing Probabilistic Risk Assessment (PRA), a systematic risk methodology for modeling the emerging risk from the interactions of equipment failure and human error in complex systems. My research focuses on performance of the humans and organizations responsible for managing, maintaining and operating complex systems, and exploring the scenarios and conditions which have the potential to create undesirable consequences for other humans, organizations, and populations.

Design, Technology and Society

The evolution of our species is inextricably linked with technology. In order to advance technologically, a certain level of risk must be taken. This scale of risk should be commensurate with social acceptance, and evaluated by the costs, effectiveness, efficiency and equity as part of a sustainable socio-technical future. The vision for utilizing risk information in decision making goes beyond its current use in high-consequence industry applications, toward a more holistic and risk-informed approach for designing and planning for the dynamic interactions of technology and society.
